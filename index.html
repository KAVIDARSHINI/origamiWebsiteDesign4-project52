Camel

  1. Camels can live around 40 - 50 years.
  2. Camels are smarter than horses.
  3. Newborn camels don't have humps. They gradually grow as they get older.
  4. Female camels are called cows and males are called bulls.

Chameleon

  1. The panther chameleon changes its color to yellow or red when it’s angry.
  2. Sick chameleons look pale in color.
  3. There are around 160 species of chameleon.
  4. The chameleons' eyes can rotate and focus separately on 180-degree arcs.

Cicada

  • Cicada can live for up to 17 years in the larva stage.
  • They have special straw-like mouths used to suck liquid from plant roots.
  • The female cicada lays 400 to 600 eggs.
  • About six to 10 weeks after the eggs are laid in cracks and holes in trees.

Panda

  • They are found in thick bamboo forests, high up in the mountains of central China
  • The giant panda’s scientific name is Ailuropoda melanoleuca, which means “black and white cat-foot”.
  • Giant pandas grow to between 1.2m and 1.5m, and weigh between 75kg and 135kg.
  • Female pandas give birth to one or two cubs every two years.

Pigeon

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities
  • Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet.

teddy Bear

  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • The first British Teddy Bear Festival was held in 1989 in London.
  • The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff.
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
